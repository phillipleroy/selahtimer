# Selah Timer Support

Welcome to Selah Timer support! This page will help you get the most out of your meeting assignment timer.

## About Selah Timer

Selah Timer is an elegant timer app designed specifically for timing midweek meeting assignments. It automatically calculates when your assignment starts based on the meeting schedule and provides a clean countdown timer to keep you perfectly on time.

---

## Getting Started

### First Time Setup

1. **Set Your Meeting Start Time**
   - When you first launch the app, you'll be asked to set when your meeting begins (typically 19:30 or 19:45)
   - This only needs to be done once, but you can change it anytime from the setup screen

2. **Choose Your Assignment**
   - Select from six assignment types:
     - Treasures from God's Word (10 min default)
     - Spiritual Gems (10 min default)
     - Bible Reading (5 min default)
     - Apply Yourself to Field Ministry (4 min default)
     - Living as Christians (10 min default)
     - Congregation Bible Study (30 min default)

3. **Review Your Start Time**
   - The app automatically calculates when your assignment begins based on the meeting schedule
   - You can customize the start time if needed by toggling "Customize start time"

4. **Adjust Duration**
   - Fine-tune the timer duration using the stepper control
   - Adjust anywhere from 1 to 60 minutes

5. **Save and Start**
   - Tap "Save and Continue" to return to the main screen
   - Your timer is now ready to use!

## Using the Timer

### Timer Controls

- **Start**: Begin the countdown when you're ready to start your assignment
- **Pause**: Temporarily stop the timer
- **Resume**: Continue the timer after pausing
- **Stop**: End the timer and reset to ready state
- **Edit**: Modify your assignment settings

### Timer Display Modes

The timer shows different information depending on its current state:

- **Ready**: Shows your configured assignment and start time, waiting for you to start
- **Running**: Active countdown with remaining time displayed prominently
- **Paused**: Timer is temporarily stopped
- **Completed**: Your assignment time has finished

### Late Start Adjustment

If you start the timer after your scheduled start time, Selah Timer automatically adjusts the remaining time to keep you on track. For example, if your assignment was supposed to start at 19:45 and you start the timer at 19:50, the app will subtract 5 minutes from your duration.

## Live Activities & Dynamic Island

Selah Timer supports Live Activities on iOS 16.2 and later, allowing you to see your countdown timer on:

- **Lock Screen**: Glance at your remaining time without unlocking your device
- **Dynamic Island** (iPhone 14 Pro and later): Monitor your timer right in the status bar area
- **Notification Center**: Swipe down to see your active timer

### Enabling Live Activities

If Live Activities aren't showing:

1. Open **Settings** on your device
2. Go to **Face ID & Passcode** (or Touch ID & Passcode)
3. Scroll to "Allow Access When Locked"
4. Enable **Live Activities**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I change my meeting start time?

1. Tap the **Edit** button in the timer section
2. Scroll to the bottom of the setup screen
3. Tap the row showing your current meeting time
4. Select your new meeting start time
5. Tap "Done"

Your assignment start times will automatically recalculate based on the new meeting time.

### Can I use this for practice at home?

Yes! Selah Timer works great for rehearsing your assignments. Just set up your assignment and start the timer whenever you're ready to practice.

### The timer doesn't match my assignment duration

Assignment durations can vary based on your congregation's needs. You can adjust the duration using the stepper control in the setup screen. The defaults are:

- Treasures: 10 minutes
- Spiritual Gems: 10 minutes
- Bible Reading: 5 minutes
- Field Ministry: 4 minutes
- Living as Christians: 10 minutes
- Congregation Bible Study: 30 minutes

### What happens if I close the app while the timer is running?

The timer continues running in the background. You can monitor it via Live Activities on your Lock Screen or Dynamic Island. When you reopen the app, it will show the current remaining time.

### Can I use this on my iPhone and iPad?

Yes! Selah Timer is a universal app that works on both iPhone and iPad running iOS 16.2 or later.

### My assignment already ended. What do I do?

If you try to start a timer for an assignment that has already passed, the app will prevent you from starting it. You have two options:

1. **Edit** the configuration to set up a different assignment or adjust the start time
2. The app will automatically be ready for your next meeting

### Does the app work offline?

Yes! Selah Timer doesn't require an internet connection. All settings are stored locally on your device.

### How do I reset the timer?

Tap the **Stop** button to end the current timer and return to the ready state. This will reset your timer to the originally configured duration without losing your assignment settings.

### The clock shows the wrong time

The clock displays your device's current time. If it's incorrect, adjust your device's time settings:

1. Open **Settings**
2. Go to **General** > **Date & Time**
3. Enable **Set Automatically**

## Troubleshooting

### The app won't start

1. Make sure you're running iOS 16.2 or later
2. Try restarting your device
3. Delete and reinstall the app

### Live Activities aren't appearing

1. Verify you're running iOS 16.2 or later
2. Check that Live Activities are enabled in Settings (see above)
3. Try stopping and restarting the timer
4. Restart the app

### The timer is counting down too fast/slow

The timer uses your device's system clock. If you notice timing issues:

1. Check that your device's time is set correctly
2. Enable **Set Automatically** in Settings > General > Date & Time

### The app crashes when I start the timer

1. Make sure you're running the latest version of iOS
2. Close other apps to free up memory
3. Restart your device
4. If the problem persists, please report it (see Contact below)
